Material Guide

Why Choose
Timber Decking?

Timber is the traditional choice for good reason. When it's the right species, properly treated and correctly installed, a timber deck performs beautifully for fifteen years or more — and it looks like what it is: a natural material that belongs in a garden.

The case for timber

  • Lower upfront cost — typically 30–50% less than premium composite
  • Natural look and feel — warm underfoot, ages with character
  • Easy to repair — individual boards can be replaced if damaged
  • Sustainably sourced — all timber from FSC-certified merchants
Full Timber Guide & FAQ →
Who Timber Suits

Homeowners who want a natural aesthetic, are comfortable with periodic oiling or treatment (every couple of years), and want to keep upfront costs down without sacrificing quality.

I'll always tell you honestly which material suits your specific situation — not whichever margin is higher.
Material Guide

Why Choose
Composite Decking?

Composite decking has come a long way. Done right, it's a serious long-term investment — near-zero maintenance, 25-year warranties, and no greying or splitting. The quality end of the market is a different thing entirely from those early plasticky products.

The case for composite

  • Near zero maintenance — no oiling, no treating. Wash it down occasionally.
  • 25-year warranties — quality boards genuinely deliver on them
  • Consistent colour — doesn't grey, silver or fade the way untreated timber does
  • Slip resistance — textured surfaces perform better in wet British conditions
Full Composite Guide & FAQ →
My Preferred Choice

Millboard

Moulded from real hardwood, not extruded — so they look genuinely like timber, not like decking trying to look like timber. They hold up, they look right, and I've never had a problem with them.

Who Composite Suits

Anyone who wants a premium, low-maintenance result and is prepared to spend more upfront. Particularly good for families, rental properties, or anyone who wants it done once and done properly.

Sourcing & Standards

Responsibly Sourced.
Properly Built.

Every piece of timber I use is sourced from FSC-certified merchants — the Forest Stewardship Council standard that guarantees the wood comes from responsibly managed forests. No illegal logging, no habitat destruction, no greenwashing.

Choosing FSC-certified timber means your garden project isn't costing the planet somewhere else. It's a standard I hold to on every job, regardless of size.

Beyond sourcing, building things properly is itself the sustainable choice. A deck or fence built correctly lasts fifteen to twenty years. A cheap job that fails in five means double the material use, double the waste, double the cost to you.

Built right the first time is the greenest thing I can do.

First conversation

Call or WhatsApp to talk through your project. No hard sell, no obligation — just a straightforward chat about what you have in mind and whether I'm the right person for the job.
